What to include in your email

Today's Tasks is operated by one person, so a little context up front saves a back-and-forth and gets you a faster reply. If you're reporting something that's broken, paste these three things into the email and I can usually reproduce on the first try:

  • Browser and version. "Chrome 125 on macOS Sonoma" is perfect. The browser console (Cmd+Option+J / F12 → Console) sometimes has a red error you can copy in.
  • What you did, in order. Two or three sentences. "Added a task to High, clicked the green check, refreshed, the completed list was empty" is far more useful than "checkmarks don't work."
  • What you expected vs what happened. This is the bit that disambiguates a bug from a feature gap.

For feature requests, please describe the problem you're trying to solve before you describe the solution you'd like. The most useful requests start with "Right now I have to ..." or "I keep finding myself ...". The smallest version of the fix is almost always different from what either of us first guessed.

Response times

I read every message that lands in the inbox, usually within a working day, and I try to reply within three. Honest answer though: Today's Tasks is a side project and I have a day job, so occasionally a message gets a slower turn-around. If you haven't heard back after a week, please nudge me — it almost certainly means your email got buried, not that I ignored it.

I don't have a support form, a chat widget, or a ticket queue. Email is the only contact channel, on purpose: it keeps the surface area small enough that I can actually keep up. There's also no automated reply, no "ticket #4729 has been opened," and no follow-up survey. You email a person; the person emails you back.

Press, partnerships, and roundups

If you're writing about productivity tools, daily planners, or no-signup web apps and you'd like a quote, a screenshot pack, or a quick interview, just say so in the subject line and I'll prioritise it. Same address — hill0225@gmail.com — works for press and partnership inquiries.

For affiliate or sponsorship pitches: I do consider them, but the bar is "does this make the site better for the reader who showed up looking for a no-signup to-do list?". Things that read as a banner-ad campaign with a press release glued on get a polite no. Things that look like a genuinely useful tool, template, or guide for the same audience get a real reply.

FAQs

Do I need an account to use the app?

No. Today’s Tasks runs entirely in your browser and stores data locally by default.

How do I move tasks to Completed?

Click the green checkmark on a task. You can restore completed items anytime.

Will you respond to every message?

We try our best. If you don’t hear back within a few days, feel free to follow up.
