What Today's Tasks is — and what it isn't

Today's Tasks is a single-page daily to-do list. Open the page, type a task, pick a lane, press add. Nothing gets installed. Nothing gets uploaded. You don't need an account. The app exists because almost every "minimal" to-do app I'd used over the previous decade ended up demanding setup time — workspaces, projects, tags, recurring rules — before it would let me write down a single task. After enough of that, I built the version I actually wanted to use, and it turned out plenty of other people wanted the same thing.

What it is: three priority lanes (High Priority, Due Today, General), a midnight reset that clears the visible list each day so stale items don't accumulate, and local-storage persistence so your data stays in your own browser. What it isn't: a project management tool, a team collaboration platform, or a notes/wiki/calendar replacement. It's deliberately a single tool that does one thing fast.

The articles linked below cover the small set of habits the app is designed around — daily intentions, time-blocking, single-task mode, and a 20-minute Friday review. Read whichever ones address your specific friction points. Or just open the app and start.
